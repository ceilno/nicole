Cashless Canteen Kiosk

Empathetic UI/UX Design and Prototyping

Project Overview

To address long queue times and order errors in school dining spaces, I designed a prototype for a Cashless Canteen Kiosk. This project relied on design-thinking principles to create an intuitive experience tailored for diverse users, from students to senior staff members.

Key Contributions

  • User Research: Created Empathy Maps and User Personas to understand user pain points, especially targeting less tech-savvy individuals.
  • Prototyping: Designed high-fidelity layouts using Adobe XD featuring clear visual systems and minimal navigation paths.
  • Usability Testing: Gathered feedback from real users to refine menu displays, ensuring high legibility and comfort.

Nursing Connection

User Empathy & Advocacy: UI/UX design is built entirely on empathy—putting oneself in the user's shoes to anticipate their physical and cognitive struggles. This mirrors nursing, where advocating for patient-centric care is the cornerstone of healing.

Accessible Communication: Creating accessible designs for seniors in this prototype taught me how physical changes (such as eyesight or memory) affect how people process information. I can leverage this knowledge to communicate care directions to geriatric patients using clear, simplified language.

Methodology

Design Thinking, Empathy Mapping, Wireframing

Tools

Adobe XD / Figma

OVERVIEW

Youth Expedition Project

A Journey That Changed My Perspective

The Youth Expedition Project (YEP) in Vietnam was a two-week overseas community service programme organised by YMCA Singapore. Together with my teammates, I travelled to Vietnam to support a local primary school through teaching, community service and cultural exchange.

Throughout the programme, I planned English lessons, taught primary school children, organised a carnival, and participated in physically demanding community work, including repairing roads outside the school.

More importantly, this experience taught me that kindness, empathy and teamwork can make a meaningful difference in the lives of others. It strengthened my passion for serving the community and played an important role in inspiring me to pursue Nursing.

ACTIVITIES

What I Did in Vietnam

Planned English Lessons

Teaching Through Creativity

Together with my teammates, we planned engaging English lessons for primary school students. We designed games, activities and simple learning materials that made lessons interactive and enjoyable while encouraging the children to participate confidently.

Taught Primary School Students

Sharing Knowledge

We conducted English lessons for the students every day. Teaching children with different learning abilities helped me become more patient, adaptable and confident when communicating with others.

Organised a Carnival

Creating Joy

Towards the end of the programme, we organised a carnival filled with games and activities for the children. Seeing their smiles and excitement reminded me that even small acts of kindness can leave a lasting impact.

Community Service

Repairing Roads

We carried heavy bags of rocks and filled potholes along the road outside the school. Although physically demanding, the experience taught me perseverance and showed me the importance of working together to improve the community.

Cultural Exchange

Learning Beyond the Classroom

Living in Vietnam for two weeks allowed me to experience a different culture and interact with local communities. I learned to respect different perspectives and adapt to unfamiliar environments with an open mind.

Working as One Team

Supporting Each Other

Throughout the expedition, teamwork was essential. We supported one another through planning, teaching, community work and problem-solving, which strengthened our communication and collaboration skills.

PERSONAL GROWTH

Skills I Developed

Compassion

Working closely with children and the local community reminded me that kindness, patience and empathy can make a meaningful difference in someone's life.

Communication

Teaching English strengthened my ability to explain ideas clearly, listen actively and communicate with people from different backgrounds.

Teamwork

Every activity required close collaboration with my teammates. Supporting one another helped us successfully complete every task throughout the expedition.

Adaptability

Living in a different country challenged me to step outside my comfort zone, adapt to new environments and embrace unfamiliar experiences with confidence.

Leadership

Through lesson planning, organising activities and supporting my teammates, I learned to take initiative, contribute ideas and encourage others.

Resilience

Whether teaching, completing physical work or overcoming unexpected challenges, I learned to remain positive, determined and focused on achieving our shared goals.

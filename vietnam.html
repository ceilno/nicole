✈️ Youth Expedition Project

YEP Vietnam

Serving Underprivileged Communities in Vietnam

Overview of the Project

Through the Youth Expedition Project (YEP) to Vietnam, I embarked on a 2-week service-learning journey to support a less privileged community. Our primary mission was to uplift the local primary school, improve their surroundings, and bring joy to the children.

Key Contributions

  • Education: Planned, designed, and taught English lessons to primary school children using interactive activities.
  • Physical Labour: Assisted in construction work by moving heavy bags of rocks to fill up holes in the muddy roads in front of the school.
  • Community Outreach: Organized a full-scale school carnival on our final days to celebrate and foster community spirit.

Nursing Connection

Patience & Communication: Teaching young children who spoke a different language pushed me to be creative with non-verbal cues and simplified visual learning. This is highly applicable to nursing, where patient teaching requires immense patience and adaptable communication styles.

Resilience & Stamina: The intense physical labour in hot weather tested my determination and stamina—qualities essential for managing the demanding, fast-paced environment of shift nursing.
